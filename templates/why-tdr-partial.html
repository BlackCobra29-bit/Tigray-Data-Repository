The TDR is more than just a hosting site. The Tigray Data Repository serves:

♦ A Bridge Between Generations:

TDR preserves history for Tigray, so its stories are also available to future generations.

♦ A Resource for Advocacy and Research:

TDR contributes to education, policy-making, and disaster responses through its collection and analysis of datasets.

♦ Accountability Tool:

The repository records hate speech, disinformation campaigns, and dehumanizing narratives that targeted Tigrayans during the active fighting of the Tigray War (2020–2022). This informs international justice investigations and accountability mechanisms.

♦ TDR's Data Analysis Journal:

TDR's data analysis publication connects Tigray with the rest of the world through its publications and serves as a center for knowledge sharing, community engagement, innovation, and collaboration.


Scope and Focus


The historical coverage by TDR includes various types of datasets about the community, such as digital, humanitarian, socio-economic, cultural, and political data. It also focuses on important events like shifts, wars, and conflicts. The aim is to bridge the gaps that have existed between war crimes and social justice so that the coverage is complete. Since the topics cover long periods and many different aspects, the effort has been made to maintain clarity and specific details throughout the coverage.

♦ Project Focus:

In the initial phase of the project, data collection will focus on the digital advocacy efforts of the Tigrayan communities living in other countries from November 2020 to November 2022. It will also focus on developing a complete profile of the Tigray Genocide victims, using the Axum Massacre as a case study. In course of time, the expansion of databases from sectors such as education, health, humanitarian affairs and many more will be facilitated in collaboration with the community and government agencies.

♦ Tigrayan Diaspora Experience:

The repository acknowledges the major role of the diaspora communities over several decades. Although the recent advocacy efforts are a major focus, TDR will also look into earlier historical periods, including the diaspora's response to the war on Tigray during the Derg regime and its effects. Through investigating these major turning points, TDR strives for an understanding of Tigray's larger history and social context: "Data for Tigray, Knowledge for Generations!"
